## 1. If Space Expands, Everything In Space Expands

Metric expansion claims that the metric tensor g_μν evolves with
time via the scale factor a(t). All distances scale by a(t).
This includes:
- Distances between galaxies
- Wavelengths of photons
- de Broglie wavelengths of massive particles
- The dimensions of atoms and rulers

The standard objection: "bound systems resist expansion through
internal forces." Atoms are held by electromagnetism. Galaxies
by gravity. Solar systems by gravity. These resist the metric
stretching and maintain their size.

But photons are NOT bound systems. They are free-propagating
waves. They have no internal force to resist the metric. So their
wavelength stretches by (1+z) — this IS the cosmological redshift.

Standard physics treats neutrinos similarly — assigning them a
de Broglie wavelength that should also stretch by (1+z).

There is no experimental evidence that a neutrino propagates as
a wave. No interference pattern. No diffraction. No observed
wavelength. The "de Broglie wavelength" is a theoretical
assignment from quantum mechanics, not a measured property.
Neutrino "oscillation" (flavor-changing over distance) is
INTERPRETED as wave interference between mass eigenstates —
but the observation is flavor change, not wave behaviour.

A neutrino is a straight-line point displacement. It has no
oscillation, no amplitude, no wavelength. The concept of
"wavelength stretching" does not apply to an entity with no
observed wave character.

## 2. The Geometric Invariance Problem

The void (empty space) has geometry. It has a metric. The
cosmological principle says this geometry is the same everywhere
(homogeneous, isotropic).

If the void is expanding, the geometry is CHANGING. The scale
factor a(t) increases. Distances grow. But:

- If rulers expand with space, the expansion is undetectable.
  Every measurement gives the same result. Nothing has changed.
- If rulers don't expand with space, then there is a PREFERRED
  FRAME — the one in which rulers are fixed while space stretches
  under them. This violates Lorentz invariance.

Standard physics solves this by saying: expansion is a GLOBAL
property of the metric, not a local one. Locally, physics is
invariant. Globally, the scale factor changes. Rulers are local
(bound by local forces), so they don't expand. Photon wavelengths
are global (they span the expanding metric), so they do.

But a photon that has traveled for 168,000 years was LOCAL at
every point along its path. It was local when it was emitted
(wavelength set by atomic physics). It was local when it was
detected (wavelength measured by a spectrometer). The "global
stretching" happened while the photon was "not being watched."

The photon's wavelength at emission: determined by local physics.
The photon's wavelength at detection: determined by local physics.
The difference: attributed to global metric change during transit.

This is not physics. This is bookkeeping.

The photon cannot resist this reshaping because it IS the

### 3.2 Neutrino: passes THROUGH the medium

The neutrino is a topological excitation with W → 0 (open
winding). It has no closed topology, no circulation, no
transverse oscillation. It is not a wave. It is a point
displacement that moves through the lattice in a straight
line. The lattice parts around it and closes behind it.

The pressure gradient cannot reshape the neutrino because
there is nothing to reshape. No amplitude to squeeze. No
wavelength to extend. No oscillation to convert. The neutrino
is not a deformation OF the lattice — it is a displacement
moving THROUGH the lattice. It has no wave character for the
convergent pressure to act on.

### 3.3 The Distinction

```
Photon:   IS the medium → medium reshapes it → redshift
Neutrino: PASSES THROUGH the medium → medium cannot reshape it → no redshift
```

This is not expansion. It is the difference between being the
wave and riding the wave.

## 4. Quantitative Consequence

### 4.1 SN 1987A (d = 168,000 ly)

Co-emitted photon and neutrino, same instant of emission:

| Messenger | Speed | Arrival delay | Energy at detection |
|-----------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Photon | c (exact) | 0 | E × (1 − z) = E × 0.999989 |
| Neutrino | c − 0.066 ms / 168,000 yr | +0.066 ms | E (unchanged) |

The neutrino arrives 0.066 ms after the photon. Both essentially
simultaneous. At this distance (z = 1.15 × 10⁻⁵), the photon
redshift is 0.00115% — unmeasurable in the neutrino spectrum
(SN neutrinos have a broad energy distribution, 5–40 MeV).

### 4.2 At Cosmological Distances

For a source at z = 1 (d ≈ 3,260 Mpc ≈ 10.6 Gly):

| Messenger | Redshift | Energy factor | Luminosity distance |
|-----------|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| Photon | z = 1 | E → E/2 | d_L = 2d |
| Neutrino | z = 0 | E → E | d_L = d |

The photon arrives with HALF its emitted energy.
The neutrino arrives with its FULL emitted energy.

The photon-derived distance is TWICE the neutrino-derived
distance. This is the fundamental SDT prediction:

```
d_L(γ) / d_L(ν) = (1 + z)
```

In standard GR: both are redshifted equally, so d_L(γ) = d_L(ν).
The ratio is 1 for all z.

### 4.3 The Test

Detect neutrinos and photons from the same cosmological source.
Measure:
1. Photon redshift z (from spectral lines)
2. Photon flux → d_L(γ)
3. Neutrino energy spectrum → compare with source spectrum
4. Neutrino flux → d_L(ν)

**SDT prediction**: neutrino energy spectrum shows NO cosmological
softening. Neutrino flux gives d_L(ν) = d_L(γ) / (1+z).

**Standard GR prediction**: neutrino energy spectrum shows SAME
softening as photons. d_L(ν) = d_L(γ).

This requires detecting neutrinos from a source with measurable
z (z > 0.01 at minimum). Current technology (IceCube, Super-K,
Hyper-K) can detect supernova neutrinos to ~10 Mpc (z = 0.002).
Next-generation detectors (DUNE, Hyper-K, JUNO) may reach 20 Mpc.

The test becomes decisive at z ~ 0.1 — a distance of ~420 Mpc.
No current or planned neutrino detector can reach this distance
for supernova neutrinos. However, high-energy astrophysical
neutrinos (from AGN jets, GRBs) have been detected at z > 0.1
by IceCube. If the source spectrum can be independently
determined, the neutrino energy at detection can test for
cosmological softening.
