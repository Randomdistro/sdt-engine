## The Core Argument

### 1. Wavelength is not just wavelength

In compressed spation, the physical length of an oscillation is altered. What we
measure in a lab as wavelength λ is the *uncompressed* value. At the emission
surface of a star, the spation is compressed — the same oscillation occupies a
different physical length in the displacement zone.

Energy per wave is conserved: if *n* energy goes into a wave of length *x*, and
the spation compression changes *x*, then the energy per wave must be preserved.
This IS the redshift — not an energy loss, but a geometric re-scaling of path
length at the origin.

**Origin direction elongation, not leading edge elongation.** The wave is
stretched uniformly from its point of emission. It doesn't stretch at the front
while the back stays fixed — the entire oscillation is re-scaled by the local
compression ratio.

### 2. Amplitude and wavelength are different things

- **Amplitude** = the depth of co-movement / harmonisation. The transverse
  component — how deeply INTO the surrounding EM field a photon couples with
  its neighbours. This is what determines *whether* a wavelength can decouple
  from the pressure regime.

- **Wavelength** = the radial structure. The longitudinal extent of one
  oscillation, which IS dependent on the compression of the displacement zone.
  In more compressed spation, the physical wavelength is shorter. When the wave
  propagates outward, it stretches.

### 4. The Dark Star Threshold

A "dark star" (SDT black hole) occurs when the displacement compression is so
extreme that **every wavelength** — including λ₀, the most transparent — is at
or below its opacity terminatum at the surface.

For the Sun:
- λ₀ decouples at R/R_sun = 0.998 (z = 1.002 × z_photosphere)
- Shorter wavelengths decouple higher up
- The Sun is NOT dark because λ₀ can still escape from below the photosphere

For a dark star:

- The "event horizon" is the radius at which z exceeds the opacity threshold
  for λ₀ — the last wavelength that could have escaped

What we call the "event horizon" in GR is, in SDT, the opacity terminatum
