## Planet Hunting Application

For any star with measured z from spectral lines, zk² = 1
gives the baseline displacement. The VARIATION in z over time
contains the planetary signal:

```
z_measured(t) = z_star + Δz_planet(t) + Δz_activity(t) + Δz_rotation

where z_star = mean measured z (from spectral lines)
      k = 1/√z_star
      κ = R·z_star

Δz_planet = periodic component = the planet
```

For a Jupiter-mass planet at 1 AU from a solar-type star:
- Radial velocity semi-amplitude: K ≈ 12.5 m/s
- Δz_doppler = K/c ≈ 4.2 × 10⁻⁸
- z_star (G2V) = 2.12 × 10⁻⁶
- Δz/z_star ≈ 2% — detectable with high-resolution spectroscopy

For an Earth-mass planet at 1 AU:
- K ≈ 0.09 m/s
- Δz_doppler ≈ 3 × 10⁻¹⁰
- Δz/z_star ≈ 0.014% — at the limit of current instruments

Once k is known, orbital velocity at any distance r:
v(r) = (c/k)·√(R/r)

The zk² = 1 relation provides the baseline. The deviation IS
the planet.
