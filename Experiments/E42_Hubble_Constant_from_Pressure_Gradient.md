# E42: Hubble Constant from Pressure Gradient

> **Category**: Cosmology
> **Difficulty**: LOW
> **Status**: Archival analysis

---

## 1. Background

The Hubble tension (73 vs 67 km/s/Mpc) is a real physical difference, not systematic error.

## 2. SDT Prediction

H₀ = v/d is a pressure gradient measure, not an expansion rate. SDT predicts H₀(near) > H₀(far).

## 3. Standard Prediction

Standard: H₀ is the expansion rate of the universe, should be constant at all distances.

## 4. Observable

Calculate H₀ from the SDT pressure gradient at different distances.

## 5. Apparatus

Existing SN Ia data (Pantheon+ catalog).

## 6. Feasibility

LOW — data reanalysis.

## 7. Impact

- **If confirmed**: Resolves the Hubble tension as a geometric effect.
- **If not confirmed**: If the gradient doesn't match: pressure model needs refinement.
