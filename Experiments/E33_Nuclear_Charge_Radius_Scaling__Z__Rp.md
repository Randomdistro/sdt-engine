# E33: Nuclear Charge Radius Scaling → Z × R_p

> **Category**: Nuclear Physics
> **Difficulty**: LOW
> **Status**: Archival analysis

---

## 1. Background

SDT predicts charge radii scale linearly with Z, not with A^(1/3).

## 2. SDT Prediction

Nuclear charge radii scale as R = Z × R_p for all elements.

## 3. Standard Prediction

Standard: R = r₀ × A^(1/3), where r₀ ≈ 1.2 fm.

## 4. Observable

Plot R_measured vs Z×R_p and vs r₀×A^(1/3) for all measured nuclei. Determine which fits better.

## 5. Apparatus

NIST nuclear charge radius database.

## 6. Feasibility

LOW — archival compilation.

## 7. Impact

- **If confirmed**: If Z-scaling fits better: invalidates the A^(1/3) model.
- **If not confirmed**: If A^(1/3) fits better: SDT's linear scaling is approximate.
