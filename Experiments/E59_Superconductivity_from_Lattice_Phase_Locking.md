# E59: Superconductivity from Lattice Phase Locking

> **Category**: Electromagnetic
> **Difficulty**: MODERATE
> **Status**: Proposed (laboratory)

---

## 1. Background

SDT reinterprets Cooper pairs as lattice-phase-locked vortex pairs. The mechanism is the same at low energy; differences appear at high-Tc.

## 2. SDT Prediction

Superconductivity is lattice phase-locking: below Tc, electron pairs lock into the spation lattice's phase structure, producing zero-resistance flow.

## 3. Standard Prediction

Standard: BCS theory — Cooper pairing via phonon exchange.

## 4. Observable

Measure vortex lattice structure in type-II superconductors at extreme resolution. Compare to SDT lattice predictions.

## 5. Apparatus

Scanning tunnelling microscopy on NbSe₂ or YBCO.

## 6. Feasibility

MODERATE — STM measurements.

## 7. Impact

- **If confirmed**: If vortex lattice matches SDT predictions: validates phase-locking model.
- **If not confirmed**: If no additional structure: BCS and SDT are indistinguishable.
