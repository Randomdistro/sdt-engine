# E72: Speed of Light Variation in Dense Media from Lattice Compression

> **Category**: Electromagnetic
> **Difficulty**: HIGH
> **Status**: Proposed (FAIR/RHIC)

---

## 1. Background

SDT predicts the same n(ω) but from a different mechanism — lattice compression changes the local relay speed.

## 2. SDT Prediction

The refractive index of dense media arises from lattice compression by nuclear occlusion.

## 3. Standard Prediction

Standard: refractive index from electromagnetic polarisation of the medium.

## 4. Observable

Measure c in extremely dense nuclear matter (neutron-rich nuclei). SDT predicts n from density.

## 5. Apparatus

Heavy-ion collision experiments.

## 6. Feasibility

HIGH — frontier nuclear physics.

## 7. Impact

- **If confirmed**: If n correlates with lattice density: validates the medium model.
- **If not confirmed**: If n follows standard polarisation: lattice compression isn't the mechanism.
