# E60: Casimir Force Scaling at Sub-nm Gaps

> **Category**: Vacuum / Medium
> **Difficulty**: MODERATE
> **Status**: Proposed (laboratory)

---

## 1. Background

SDT predicts the Casimir force ultimately resolves into individual spation relay contributions at sub-nm gaps. The force law should show discretisation effects.

## 2. SDT Prediction

Casimir force at sub-nm gaps should show deviations from the 1/d⁴ power law due to spation lattice granularity.

## 3. Standard Prediction

Standard: Casimir force scales as 1/d⁴ at all separable distances.

## 4. Observable

Measure Casimir force at 0.1-10 nm plate separations with atomic force microscopy.

## 5. Apparatus

AFM with calibrated cantilever, gold surfaces.

## 6. Feasibility

MODERATE — AFM exists but sub-nm Casimir is at the measurement frontier.

## 7. Impact

- **If confirmed**: Force discretisation would directly image spation lattice structure.
- **If not confirmed**: If smooth 1/d⁴: lattice granularity is below AFM resolution.
