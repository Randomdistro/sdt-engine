# E44: CMB Dipole from Observer Motion in Pressure Field

> **Category**: Cosmology
> **Difficulty**: LOW
> **Status**: Archival analysis

---

## 1. Background

SDT and standard physics agree on the kinematic interpretation but differ on ontology: SDT says we're moving through a real medium.

## 2. SDT Prediction

The CMB dipole (370 km/s) reflects our motion through the pressure field, not relative to a "rest frame" of expansion.

## 3. Standard Prediction

Standard: CMB dipole from Earth's peculiar velocity relative to the CMB rest frame.

## 4. Observable

Measure higher-order CMB motion effects (e.g., aberration quadrupole) that distinguish medium-motion from frame-motion.

## 5. Apparatus

Planck CMB data.

## 6. Feasibility

LOW — archival.

## 7. Impact

- **If confirmed**: Higher-order effects could distinguish medium from frame.
- **If not confirmed**: If indistinguishable: both interpretations are observationally equivalent at current precision.
