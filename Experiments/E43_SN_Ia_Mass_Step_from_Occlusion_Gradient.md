# E43: SN Ia Mass Step from Occlusion Gradient

> **Category**: Cosmology
> **Difficulty**: LOW
> **Status**: Archival analysis

---

## 1. Background

High-mass galaxies have higher local convergence (denser environments). This produces a systematic brightness offset in hosted supernovae.

## 2. SDT Prediction

The ~0.04 mag mass step in SN Ia standardisation arises from host-galaxy convergence field differences.

## 3. Standard Prediction

Standard: dust, metallicity, or progenitor differences.

## 4. Observable

Correlate mass-step residuals with galaxy density measures.

## 5. Apparatus

Pantheon+ catalog, host galaxy data.

## 6. Feasibility

LOW — archival.

## 7. Impact

- **If confirmed**: SDT explanation of the mass step would be a major cosmological result.
- **If not confirmed**: If correlations don't hold: mass step has other origins.
