## Why Neon is Impervious

The cubic deuteron shell is CLOSED. There are no geometric voids for additional deuterons or tritons to occupy without starting a new shell. This is why:

1. **Neon has the highest ionisation energy of Period 2** (21.56 eV): the electronic cube mirrors the nuclear cube — both are filled, serene, impervious
2. **Neon is chemically inert**: no geometric stress, no electron vacuum, no reactivity
3. **Neon resists further nuclear construction**: adding a deuteron or triton to Ne-20 means starting a third shell, which requires overcoming the cubic closure energy barrier

This imperviousness is why neon is so abundant cosmically (3.44 × 10⁶ relative to Si = 10⁶) while sodium is ~57× rarer: neon resists being processed into sodium.

## Construction Chains

### Parents
- **F-19 + d → Ne-21** (NOT Ne-20 — the deuteron addition to fluorine goes to 21, not 20)
- **F-19 + t → Ne-22** (triton addition to fluorine goes to 22)
- **Ne-20 is built primarily by oxygen/neon burning in massive stellar cores** (O-16 + α → Ne-20, which in grammar terms adds 2 deuterons to oxygen's 6, reaching 8)

### Children
- **Ne-20 + t → Na-23** (100% of sodium) — the only stable sodium comes from the dominant neon
- Ne-20 is virtually impervious to deuteron capture at normal stellar temperatures

### The Neon-Sodium Barrier

Ne-20 → Na-23 requires triton addition. This is energetically expensive because it means cracking the cubic closure. Only in the extreme conditions of neon burning (T > 1.2 × 10⁹ K) can this barrier be overcome. This is why sodium is rare.
