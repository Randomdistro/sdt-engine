# Lithium-6 — ⁶Li

**The Alpha-Deuteron Dumbbell. The Minority Lithium.**

- **Z**: 3 (3 protons)
- **A**: 6 (6 nucleons)
- **N**: 3 (3 neutrons)
- **Abundance**: 7.59% of all lithium
- **Stability**: Stable
- **Binding Energy**: 31.994 MeV (5.332 MeV/nucleon)

---

## Nuclear Construction Grammar

| Component | Count |
|-----------|-------|
| Alpha core | 1 |
| Deuterons | 1 |
| Tritons | 0 |

$$A = 4 + 2(1) + 3(0) = 6 \quad Z = 2 + 1 + 0 = 3 \checkmark$$

Li-6 is the **simplest possible nuclear construction**: one alpha core plus one deuteron. The deuteron caps one face of the alpha tetrahedron, creating a prolate (dumbbell) shape.

**Symmetry score**: 100 (pure deuteron — maximum symmetry for 1 block). Yet Li-6 is NOT the dominant isotope (7.59% vs Li-7's 92.41%). This is the first case where the triton-bearing isotope is more abundant than the pure-deuteron form — explained by the triton's additional binding energy from its extra neutron providing geometric stability.

## Construction Chains

### Parents
- **He-4 + d → Li-6** (alpha captures a deuteron)
- **He-3 + t → Li-6** (minor pathway)

### Children
- **Li-6 + d → Be-8** → UNSTABLE (splits to 2α in 10⁻¹⁶ s). Dead end.
- **Li-6 + t → Be-9** (100% of beryllium) — **productive pathway**
- **Li-6 + n → T + α** (tritium breeding reaction — used in fusion reactor blankets)

### Feedstock Role

Li-6 is valuable feedstock for beryllium production (Li-6 + t → Be-9) and tritium breeding (Li-6 + n → T + α). Its relative rarity (7.59%) reflects partial consumption in these processes, not difficulty of production.

## SDT Characterisation

### Nuclear Geometry

The alpha tetrahedron + one deuteron cap:
- **Shape**: Prolate spheroid (elongated along the alpha-deuteron axis)
- **Spin**: 1+ (the deuteron's spin is not cancelled by the alpha's zero spin)
- **Magnetic moment**: +0.822 μ_N
- **Quadrupole moment**: −0.083 mb (slight oblate distortion from the cap)

## Cosmic Significance

- **Primordial production**: Li-6 is produced in trace amounts during the pre-Clearing nucleosynthesis epoch
- **Stellar destruction**: Li-6 is destroyed at temperatures above ~2 × 10⁶ K (even cooler than Li-7 destruction temperature), so it does not survive passage through stellar interiors
- **Be-8 dead end**: Li-6 + d → Be-8 is a "no-show" — Be-8 instantly splits back into two alphas. This dead end is why beryllium exists only as Be-9 (requiring the triton pathway from Li-7 or the triton pathway from Li-6)
