## SDT Characterisation

### The Perfect Tetrahedron

The alpha particle is the geometric fusion of two deuterons. The four nucleons (2p + 2n) lock into a tetrahedral configuration:

- **2 protons** (W=3 trefoils) at two vertices, L-chirality
- **2 neutrons** (W=3 trefoils with internal electron) at two vertices, R-chirality
- **Geometry**: Perfect tetrahedron, all 6 pair distances equal
- **Spin**: 0 (all angular momenta cancel by symmetry)
- **Magnetic moment**: 0 (all wake fields cancel)
- **Electric quadrupole**: 0 (spherical to first order)

This is why the alpha is "serene" — every geometric excess is cancelled. No net spin, no net magnetism, no distortion. A perfect ball bearing.

### Binding Energy

28.3 MeV for 4 nucleons = 7.07 MeV/nucleon. This is the **anomalously high** binding energy for such a light nucleus. In SDT terms: the tetrahedral geometry allows all four nucleons to share a single circulation cell. The two internal electrons (one from each neutron) weave between all four nucleons simultaneously, creating a "super-stabilised" knot that cannot be unwound without extreme energy input.

### Why It Doesn't Decay

The alpha has topological protection: the tetrahedral trefoil quartet is a closed circulation that cannot be continuously deformed into a simpler topology. You cannot remove one nucleon without breaking the entire circulation pattern. This is why alpha emission is the preferred decay mode for heavy nuclei — the alpha comes out whole because it cannot come out any other way.

## Family Role

### As Core

Every nucleus with Z ≥ 2 has exactly one alpha at its centre:
- Li-6 = α + 1d
- Li-7 = α + 1t
- C-12 = α + 4d
- O-16 = α + 6d
- Fe-56 = α + 20d + 4t
- U-238 = α + 36d + 54t

### As Emission Product

When heavy nuclei shed excess triton bonds, they do so as alpha particles. Each emitted alpha carries away 2 protons and 2 neutrons — equivalent to removing 1 deuteron + 1 triton from the construction (or equivalently, one d-t pair).

### Construction Chains

- He-4 + d → Li-6 (7.59% of lithium)
- He-4 + t → Li-7 (92.41% of lithium) — **dominant**
- Triple-alpha: 3 × He-4 → C-12 + γ (stellar helium burning — but in SDT grammar, C-12 is α + 4d, not 3α. The "triple alpha" process produces an alpha core plus 4 deuterons from the breakdown and reconfiguration of 2 additional alphas)

## Electronic Structure

| Level | Energy (eV) | k-factor | Description |
|-------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| 1 (1s²) | 24.587 | 101.9 | **The Fortress Wall.** Two electrons meshed as counter-rotating W=1 dyad. Nearly double hydrogen's ionisation energy because the meshed pair stabilise each other. |
| 2 (1s¹) | 54.418 | 68.5 | **Z² verification.** 13.6 × 4 = 54.4 eV. Perfect hydrogen-like scaling for the stripped ion. |

### Parahelium vs Orthohelium

- **Parahelium** (singlet, antiparallel spins): Counter-rotating W=1 vortices mesh like gears → constructive laminar flow → ground state
- **Orthohelium** (triplet, parallel spins): Same-direction vortices clash → destructive turbulence → metastable, ~20 eV higher

This is the mechanical basis of electron pairing. Not an abstract exclusion principle — a gear-meshing condition.
