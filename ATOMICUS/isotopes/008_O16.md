# Oxygen-16 — ¹⁶O

**The Octahedral Shell. NOT Four Alphas.**

- **Z**: 8 (8 protons)
- **A**: 16 (16 nucleons)
- **N**: 8 (8 neutrons)
- **Abundance**: 99.757% of all oxygen
- **Stability**: Stable (doubly magic: Z=8, N=8)
- **Binding Energy**: 127.619 MeV (7.976 MeV/nucleon)

---

## Nuclear Construction Grammar

| Component | Count |
|-----------|-------|
| Alpha core | 1 |
| Deuterons | 6 |
| Tritons | 0 |

$$A = 4 + 2(6) + 3(0) = 16 \quad Z = 2 + 6 + 0 = 8 \checkmark$$

O-16 is alpha + 6 deuterons, zero tritons. The 6 deuterons arrange around the alpha core in an **octahedral shell**: one deuteron on each face of the dual geometry created by the inner tetrahedron. This is the most symmetric arrangement possible for 6 objects around a central core.

**Symmetry score**: 100 (pure deuteron — maximum).

### Correction from Legacy
O-16 is NOT 4 alpha particles. It is 1 alpha core with 6 deuterons. The "4α" appearance is because adjacent pairs of deuterons present alpha-like 2p+2n content, but they are bound by the central alpha's convergence field, not as independent alpha units.

## Construction Chains

### Parents
- **N-14 + d → O-16** (dominant — N-14 is 99.6% of nitrogen, captures deuteron)
- **C-12 + α → O-16 + γ** (carbon burning — adds 2 deuterons' worth of nucleons)

### Children
- **O-16 + d → F-18** (unstable, β⁺ → O-18)
- **O-16 + t → F-19** (100% of fluorine) — this is the ONLY pathway to stable fluorine
- **O-16 + α → Ne-20** (oxygen/neon burning — adds to neon construction)

### The Fluorine Bottleneck
All stable fluorine (F-19) must come from O-16 + triton. Since fluorine has only ONE stable isotope and it requires triton capture, fluorine production is rate-limited by triton availability at stellar core temperatures. This is why fluorine is remarkably rare (cosmic abundance only 843 relative to Si = 10⁶).

## SDT Characterisation

### Nuclear Geometry
- **Structure**: Alpha tetrahedron + 6 deuterons in octahedral arrangement
- **Doubly magic**: In SDT terms, both the proton count (8 = filled cube) and neutron count (8) achieve geometric closure. The octahedral deuteron shell is "full."
- **Spin**: 0 (octahedral symmetry cancels everything)
- **Magnetic moment**: 0
- **Quadrupole moment**: 0

Like C-12, O-16 is geometrically "finished." But where C-12 has tetrahedral deuteron arrangement, O-16 has octahedral — a higher symmetry group.

### Binding Energy
7.976 MeV/nucleon — higher than C-12's 7.680 MeV/nucleon. The octahedral arrangement is more tightly bound than the tetrahedral one, because each deuteron has more nearest neighbours sharing convergence.

## Isotope Siblings

### O-17 (0.038% — THE RAREST COMMON ISOTOPE)

| Component | Count |
|-----------|-------|
| Alpha core | 1 |
| Deuterons | 5 |
| Tritons | 1 |

One triton replaces a deuteron. Symmetry score: 59 (odd triton). This is the rarest naturally occurring oxygen isotope, and one of the rarest stable isotopes of ANY abundant element. The single triton shatters the octahedral perfection.

- **Spin**: 5/2⁻ (the triton breaks zero-spin symmetry dramatically)
- **Construction**: N-14 + t → O-17 OR N-15 + d → O-17
- **Feedstock**: O-17 is consumed in stellar cores by O-17 + p → N-14 + α (CNO cycle)

### O-18 (0.205% — OPPOSING TRITONS RESTORE SYMMETRY)

| Component | Count |
|-----------|-------|
| Alpha core | 1 |
| Deuterons | 4 |
| Tritons | 2 |

Two tritons replace two deuterons. Symmetry score: 78 (even triton, partially restored). If the two tritons sit on opposing faces of the octahedron, the symmetry is partially recovered — which is exactly why O-18 is 5.4× more abundant than O-17 despite having MORE tritons.

$$\frac{\text{O-18}}{\text{O-17}} = \frac{0.205}{0.038} = 5.4$$

This ratio is the **opposing-triton symmetry test** in its clearest form. Two tritons (partially symmetric) > one triton (maximally asymmetric). The grammar predicts; nature confirms.

- **Spin**: 0 (opposing tritons cancel — symmetry restored!)
- **Construction**: N-15 + t → O-18

## Cosmic Census

- **By mass**: ~1.04% of baryonic matter (3rd most abundant element)
- **Production**: Stellar helium burning, carbon burning, neon burning
- **Solar**: O-16 is the most abundant element in Earth's crust (46% by mass)
- **Water**: Every water molecule contains one oxygen — O-16 dominates
