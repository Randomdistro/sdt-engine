# Deuterium — ²H (D)

**The First Bond. The Mortar of All Nuclei.**

- **Z**: 1 (1 proton)
- **A**: 2 (2 nucleons)
- **N**: 1 (1 neutron)
- **Abundance**: 0.0115% of all hydrogen
- **Stability**: Stable
- **Binding Energy**: 2.224 MeV

Deuterium IS one deuteron. It is the smallest nuclear construction — one proton bound to one neutron by convergent boundary pressure. The neutron itself contains an internal electron (n = p⁺ + e⁻_int); this internal electron circulates between the two protons' wakes, binding them.

The deuteron is the **mortar** of all nuclear construction. Every nucleus with Z ≥ 2 contains deuterons as structural elements. When we say "add a deuteron" in the familial chain, we mean: attach one proton-neutron pair to the existing construction.

## SDT Characterisation

### Binding Mechanism

The deuteron's 2.224 MeV binding energy is the cost of separating the two nucleon vortices once their wakes have meshed. The internal electron acts as a geometric bridge — its W=1 circulation weaves between both W=3 proton trefoils, creating a coupled system.

### Spin and Moment

- **Spin**: 1 (the proton and neutron spins align parallel)
- **Magnetic moment**: 0.857 μ_N (sum of proton + neutron moments in aligned configuration)
- **Quadrupole moment**: +2.86 mb (the deuteron is slightly prolate — the two nucleons don't sit concentrically but form a dumbbell)

The non-zero quadrupole moment proves the deuteron is NOT a point particle. It has geometric extension — SDT predicts exactly this from the wake coupling geometry.

## Family Role

### As Building Block

The deuteron is the **primary construction unit** in stellar nucleosynthesis:

- D + D → He-3 + n (releasing one neutron that quickly re-pairs)
- D + D → T + p (creating a triton)
- D + T → He-4 + n (the easiest fusion reaction — the basis of tokamak designs)

### As Feedstock

Deuterium is produced primordially and consumed in stars. Its cosmic abundance (D/H ≈ 2.5 × 10⁻⁵) is a **one-way ratchet**: deuterium is destroyed in stellar cores and never replenished. Its current abundance is a direct measure of how much stellar processing has occurred.

### Construction Chains

- H-1 + H-1 → D + e⁺ + ν_e (the pp chain: two protons forced together, one converts to neutron via beta-plus, neutrino receipt filed)
- D as additive: Parent(Z-1) + d → Child(Z, A+2) — this is the primary familial construction step

## Cosmic Census

- **Primordial production**: ~25 deuterons per million protons (D/H ≈ 2.5 × 10⁻⁵)
- **Current**: depleted by stellar processing
- **Significance**: The D/H ratio is one of the strongest constraints on the density of baryonic matter, because deuterium is fragile — it is destroyed at temperatures above ~5 × 10⁵ K (any stellar interior)
- **Neutrino signature**: 1 receipt filed per deuteron created from pp fusion

## Spectral Signature

Deuterium has a slightly different Rydberg constant due to its doubled nuclear mass. The isotope shift is detectable:

| Line | D wavelength | H wavelength | Shift |
|------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Balmer-α | 656.10 nm | 656.28 nm | −0.18 nm |

This shift was how deuterium was discovered (Urey, 1931). In SDT terms: the heavier nucleus moves more slowly in the convergence field, changing the electron's orbital radius slightly.
