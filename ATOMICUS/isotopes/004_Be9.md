## Nuclear Construction Grammar

| Component | Count |
|-----------|-------|
| Alpha core | 1 |
| Deuterons | 1 |
| Tritons | 1 |

$$A = 4 + 2(1) + 3(1) = 9 \quad Z = 2 + 1 + 1 = 4 \checkmark$$

Be-9 is the **first mixed construction**: alpha + 1 deuteron + 1 triton. The deuteron and triton cap two faces of the alpha tetrahedron, creating a compact but asymmetric assembly.

**The Be-8 No-Show**: Be-8 (α + 2d, or equivalently 2α) is unstable — it splits back into two alpha particles within 6.7 × 10⁻¹⁷ seconds. This is the most famous nuclear instability: two alphas cannot bind without a triton bridge. Be-9 exists BECAUSE the triton provides the geometric asymmetry that prevents the two-alpha resonance from decomposing.

Be-9 is built from TWO convergent pathways:
- Li-7 (α + 1t) + d → Be-9 (α + 1d + 1t)
- Li-6 (α + 1d) + t → Be-9 (α + 1d + 1t)

Both lithium isotopes converge on the same beryllium. This is construction grammar at work: two different parent families, one stable child.

## Why Be-8 Fails and Be-9 Survives

Be-8 = α + 2d. This is equivalent to two alpha particles (each alpha = 1 alpha decomposed as 2 deuterons). Two alphas approach each other, their tetrahedral structures try to mesh, but the convergence geometry creates a symmetric repulsion — they bounce apart. The symmetry is the problem: two identical objects cannot lock without a mediating asymmetry.

Be-9 = α + 1d + 1t. The triton's extra neutron breaks the symmetry. It acts as a geometric wedge that prevents the would-be alpha pair from separating. The triton bridge is what makes beryllium possible.

This is why Be-9 is the **bottleneck of stellar nucleosynthesis**. All construction beyond lithium must pass through this one isotope. If Be-9 didn't exist (if the triton bridge didn't work), the universe would contain nothing heavier than lithium.

## Construction Chains

### Parents
- **Li-7 + d → Be-9** (dominant — Li-7 is 92.4% of lithium)
- **Li-6 + t → Be-9** (secondary — convergent pathway)

### Children
- **Be-9 + d → B-11** (80.1% of boron) — **dominant boron pathway**
- **Be-9 + α → C-12 + n** (a carbon production route)

### The Bottleneck

Be-9 is mono-isotopic — there is no other stable beryllium. Every atom of carbon, nitrogen, oxygen, iron, gold, uranium in the universe passed through Be-9. It is the **eye of the needle**.

## Cosmic Census

- **Abundance**: Very rare (~2.6 × 10⁻¹¹ relative to H)
- **Production**: Primarily cosmic ray spallation (high-energy protons smashing carbon/oxygen nuclei in interstellar medium)
- **NOT produced in stars**: Stars destroy beryllium (too fragile at stellar temperatures)
- **Significance**: Its rarity reflects the bottleneck — Be-9 is the narrow gate, but most of what passes through is immediately processed into boron and carbon
