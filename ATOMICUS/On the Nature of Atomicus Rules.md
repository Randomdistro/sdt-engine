## I. The Nuclear Laws (The Architecture of the Core)

### 1. The Alpha Core

Every nucleus has exactly **one alpha particle** ($^4$He) at its centre. The alpha is the irreducible nuclear unit: a perfect tetrahedral assembly of 2 protons and 2 neutrons, a W=3 trefoil quartet with 4 nucleons sharing a single toroidal circulation cell. Binding energy = 28.3 MeV per alpha.

**Correction from v5.0**: Nuclei are NOT built by "stacking alpha bricks." Carbon-12 is NOT 3 alphas. Oxygen-16 is NOT 4 alphas. Neon-20 is NOT 5 alphas. Each nucleus has ONE alpha core; all additional nucleons are organised as deuterons and tritons around that core.

### 2. The Deuteron-Triton Code (No Free Neutrons)

All nuclear construction beyond the alpha core uses exactly two building blocks:
- **Deuteron** (d): 1 proton + 1 neutron (A=2, Z=1). The structural mortar.
- **Triton** (t): 1 proton + 2 neutrons (A=3, Z=1). The weighted wedge.

Neutrons NEVER appear alone in stable nuclei. Every neutron is bound within a deuteron or triton formation. A "free neutron" is an exposed triton remnant — it decays in 10.2 minutes because it lacks the geometric shelter of a paired formation.

**Decomposition rule**: For any isotope with $Z \geq 2$:
$$n_{\text{tri}} = A - 2Z$$
$$n_{\text{deu}} = 3Z - A - 2$$
$$A = 4 + 2n_{\text{deu}} + 3n_{\text{tri}}, \quad Z = 2 + n_{\text{deu}} + n_{\text{tri}}$$

This decomposition is unique for every isotope. Coverage: 98.7% of all stable isotopes Z=1–30. The sole edge case is He-3 (not decomposable — it is a proto-alpha missing one neutron).

### 3. Symmetry Determines Abundance

The most abundant isotope of every element is the one with the **highest geometric symmetry** in its deu/tri arrangement around the alpha core:

- **Pure deuteron** (n_tri = 0): Maximum symmetry → highest abundance. Examples: C-12, N-14, O-16, Ne-20, Si-28, S-32, Ca-40.
- **Even triton count** (n_tri = 2, 4, 6...): Opposing tritons restore partial symmetry → second tier. Examples: O-18 > O-17, Ne-22 > Ne-21, Mg-26 > Mg-25.
- **Odd triton count** (n_tri = 1, 3, 5...): Maximum asymmetry → lowest abundance for the family. Examples: O-17 (0.038%), Ne-21 (0.27%), N-15 (0.368%).

Symmetry predicts the most abundant isotope correctly in 64% of multi-isotope elements (Z=1–30). The exceptions (Li, B, Ar, K, Ti, V, Cr, Fe) have additional mechanisms (feedstock consumption, stellar processing, decay chains) that override raw symmetry.

### 4. Familial Construction Chains

Each element Z builds from its predecessor Z-1 by the addition of exactly one deuteron or one triton:

$$\text{Parent}(Z-1, A_p) + d \to \text{Child}(Z, A_p+2)$$
$$\text{Parent}(Z-1, A_p) + t \to \text{Child}(Z, A_p+3)$$

This is the stellar nucleosynthesis mechanism: every fusion event is the attachment of one deu or tri to an existing construction. The pathway determines which child isotope is produced.

Key verified chains:
- He-4 + t → Li-7 (dominant lithium, 92.4%)
- Be-9 + d → B-11 (dominant boron, 80.1%)
- B-10 + d → C-12 (dominant carbon, 98.9%)
- C-12 + d → N-14 (dominant nitrogen, 99.6%)
- N-14 + d → O-16 (dominant oxygen, 99.8%)
- O-16 + t → F-19 (only stable fluorine, 100%)
- Ne-21 + d → Na-23 (only stable sodium, 100%)
- Si-28 + t → P-31 (only stable phosphorus, 100%)

### 5. Feedstock Consumption

A parent isotope's current abundance reflects **how much was left over** after stellar processing consumed the rest:

- Ne-21 at 0.27% of neon: original production was ~2.0%. 87% was consumed building Na-23.
- N-15 at 0.368% of nitrogen: much was consumed building O-17.
- Rare isotopes are not rare because they are hard to make. They are rare because they are **useful — and mostly used**.

### 6. The Triton Fraction Spectrum

The ratio of tritons to total blocks (d+t) determines an element's position on the stability spectrum:

| Regime | Triton fraction | Character |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| Light (He–Ne) | 0–12% | Deuteron-dominated, highly stable |

Heavy element radioactivity = **triton overload**. ### 7. The Neutrino Receipt

Every nuclear state change — every fusion, fission, or beta transition — files an **open-winding receipt**: a neutrino. The neutrino carries the topological residue of the winding reconfiguration:

- $\beta^-$: n → p + e + $\bar{\nu}_e$ (triton loses a neutron → becomes deuteron, receipt filed)
- $\beta^+$: p → n + e$^+$ + $\nu_e$ (deuteron gains a neutron → becomes triton, receipt filed)
- pp chain: 4p → $^4$He + 2e$^+$ + 2$\nu_e$ + 26.7 MeV (2 receipts per alpha built)

The cosmic neutrino-to-baryon ratio ($\sim 10^9$) is the total receipt count of the universe's matter construction history.
