## Definition

L/K ratio = (L-shell capture rate) / (K-shell capture rate)

K-shell (1s): innermost, highest electron density at nucleus
L-shell (2s/2p): second shell, lower density
M-shell (3s/3p/3d): third shell, much lower density

## Measured L/K Ratios by Element

### Beryllium-7 (Z=4)

| Environment | L/K ratio | K% | L% | Source |
|-------------|-----------|-----|-----|--------|
| Theoretical (free atom) | 0.09–0.11 | 90–92 | 8–10 | Bambynek tables |
| Cryogenic microcalorimeter | 0.040 ± 0.006 | ~96 | ~4 | Experimental |
| Embedded in Ta metal | 0.070 ± 0.007 | ~93 | ~7 | Experimental |
| BeO ceramic | 0.055–0.065 | ~94 | ~6 | Experimental |
| **Variation range** | **0.040–0.070** | **93–96** | **4–7** | — |

**Conclusion**: K-capture is constant (~93–96%). L-capture varies ×1.75
with chemical environment. The core parahelium is mechanically locked;
the valence coupling is chemically tunable.

### General trend

| Z | Element | L/K ratio | K% |
|---|---------|-----------|-----|
| 4 | Be | 0.04–0.07 | 93–96 |
| 18 | Ar | 0.09 | 92 |
| 26 | Fe | 0.107 | 90 |
| 32 | Ge | 0.114 | 87 |
| 48 | Cd | 0.152 | 87 |
| 54 | Xe | 0.17 | 85 |
| 56 | Ba | 0.18 | 84 |

**L/K ratio increases monotonically with Z.** As Z increases, the
L-shell electrons get closer to the nucleus (contracted by higher
nuclear charge), increasing their coupling strength relative to
K-electrons. But K-capture ALWAYS dominates (>80% for all measured
elements up to Z=56).

## SDT Interpretation

The K-electrons are the parahelium dyad — the deepest electronic
gear, directly meshed to the alpha core. Their capture rate is
governed by the nuclear convergence field strength, which is
Z-dependent but environment-independent.

The L-electrons are the first valence gear layer. Their coupling
is modulated by the chemical bonds (metallic, ionic, covalent),
which alter the electron density at the nuclear surface.

Higher-Z elements have more contracted L-shells (higher effective
nuclear charge), so the L-shell progressively approaches the K-shell
in coupling strength. But the K-shell's direct core coupling
maintains dominance across all measured elements.
