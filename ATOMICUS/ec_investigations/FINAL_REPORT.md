## Abstract

Electron Capture (EC) is the nuclear construction grammar's self-correction
mechanism. Every EC event performs the identical grammar operation:
Δn_tri = +2, Δn_deu = −3 (gain two tritons, lose three deuterons). This
universal identity holds without exception across 56 tested isotopes from
Z=4 (beryllium) to Z=83 (bismuth). EC converts grammar-invalid or
grammar-suboptimal nuclear scaffolds into grammar-valid or grammar-superior
configurations. The nuclear gear train either PROTECTS the defective proton
(pure EC, protection factors up to 10¹²) or EJECTS it (β⁺, anti-protection
factor < 1), depending on the grammar penalty. The investigation identifies
Be-7 as the fundamental unit of EC, discovers the ~35-day half-life cluster
at subshell closures, explains the missing elements (Tc, Pm) via odd-block
scaffold competition, and establishes energy ratio connections between
electronic and nuclear gear-flip scales.
