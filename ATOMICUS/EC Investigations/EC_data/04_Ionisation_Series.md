## Purpose

Full ionisation series for every element that sits at a subshell closure
AND has a pure EC isotope. The gap between successive ionisations reveals
the shell structure: large gaps = core breach.

All values in eV. Source: NIST Atomic Spectra Database.

## Core Breach Ratio Summary

| Closure | Z | IE₃/IE₂ | SDT shell | Pattern |
|---------|---|---------|-----------|---------|
| 2s² | 4 (Be) | **8.46** | 2→1 | HUGE (alpha core is sacrosanct) |
| 3p⁶ | 18 (Ar) | 1.37 | 3→2 | Moderate |
| 4s² | 20 (Ca) | **4.28** | 4→3 | Large (noble gas core below) |
| 4d¹⁰ | 48 (Cd) | 2.22 | 5→4 | Moderate |
| 6s² | 56 (Ba) | **3.58** | 6→5 | Large |
| 4f¹⁴ | 70 (Yb) | 2.06 | f→d transition | Sharp |
| 5d¹⁰ | 80 (Hg) | 1.82 | 6→5d | Lowest ratio |

**Pattern**: Core breach ratio DECREASES with Z. Heavier elements have
more diffuse shell boundaries — the gap between shell layers shrinks
as more electrons screen the nuclear charge. Be's alpha core is by
far the most sharply defined boundary (8.46× jump).

## √(24,117) vs IE₃ Check

| Closure | IE₃ (eV) | √24,117 = 155.3 | IE₃/155.3 |
|---------|----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Be** | **153.9** | **155.3** | **0.991** |
| Ca | 50.9 | 155.3 | 0.328 |
| Cd | 37.5 | 155.3 | 0.241 |
| Ba | 35.8 | 155.3 | 0.231 |
| Yb | 25.1 | 155.3 | 0.161 |
| Hg | 34.2 | 155.3 | 0.220 |

**Only Be matches.** The √24,117 ≈ IE₃ relationship is SPECIFIC to
beryllium. It does not generalise to other closures. This is because
the 478 keV gamma and 19.82 eV orthohelium transition are both specific
to the helium/lithium/beryllium system.
