## Key Observations

### 1. mp/me × Z matches ONLY for Be-7 and V-49

| Isotope | Protection | mp/me × Z | Ratio |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **Be-7** | 7,514 | 7,345 | **1.023** (2.3% match) |
| **V-49** | 46,585 | 42,232 | **1.103** (10.3%) |

No other isotope comes close. The mp/me × Z formula is NOT a universal scaling.

### 2. Low-Q isotopes have enormous protection

| Isotope | Q (keV) | Protection |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| Ho-163 | 2.8 | 2.4 × 10⁸ |
| Pb-205 | 51 | 7.9 × 10¹¹ |
| Pt-193 | 56 | 2.6 × 10⁶ |
| Ge-68 | 107 | 38,251 |
| Ta-179 | 110 | 93,824 |

Protection correlates strongly with 1/Q (low energy available = slow decay).

### 3. The Q-normalised protection

If decay rate ∝ Q², then Q-normalised protection ∝ Protection × Q²:

| Isotope | Protection | Q (keV) | Prot × Q² | Z |
|---------|-----------|---------|-----------|---|
| Be-7 | 7,514 | 862 | 5.58 × 10⁹ | 4 |
| Ar-37 | 4,947 | 814 | 3.28 × 10⁹ | 18 |
| Fe-55 | 141,470 | 231 | 7.55 × 10⁹ | 26 |
| Co-57 | 38,368 | 836 | 2.68 × 10¹⁰ | 27 |
| Xe-127 | 5,131 | 662 | 2.25 × 10⁹ | 54 |
| Yb-169 | 4,522 | 909 | 3.73 × 10⁹ | 70 |

The Q-normalised protection spans a range of ~10× (2.25–26.8 × 10⁹),
which is far tighter than the raw protection (which spans 10⁸×). The
Q² normalisation partially accounts for the variation, but does not
collapse it to a single value. The residual variation encodes nuclear
matrix element differences (scaffold geometry effects).
