### On the Locked Gear: Nitrogen-14

**Abstract**
Nitrogen-14 (99.6%) is an anomaly. It is one of the very few stable "Odd-Odd" nuclei (7 protons, 7 neutrons). Usually, unpaired protons and neutrons are unstable ($^{2}H$, $^{6}Li$, $^{10}B$, $^{14}N$ are the only light stable ones).

**The Mechanism of Odd-Odd Stability**
-   **Spin 1:** The nucleus has a net integer spin (1).
-   **Deuteron Coupling:** SDT proposes that the nucleus is structured as a Carbon-12 Core + a Deuteron (p-n pair) pivoting on the surface.
-   **The "Locked Gear":** The Carbon-12 core is spin 0 (Triangle). The Deuteron is spin 1. The stability comes from the fact that the Deuteron "locks" into the triangular face of the C-12 core perfectly, creating a 7-nucleon symmetric stack that is effectively spherical.
-   **Proof:** If you add one neutron ($^{15}N$), the geometry breaks, spin becomes 1/2, and the "Locked Gear" mechanism is lost, making $^{15}N$ much rarer.
